Natural Wealth of Tamil Nadu

Discover the scenic beauty, wildlife, and heritage that make Tamil Nadu a natural treasure.

1. Mudumalai National Park


Nestled at the tri-junction of Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Karnataka, Mudumalai National Park is part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve. It’s home to elephants, tigers, leopards, and more than 250 bird species.


Mudumalai National Park

Quick Facts
Location Nilgiri District, Tamil Nadu, India
Established 1940 (Declared as a Wildlife Sanctuary), later designated as a National Park in 1990
Coordinates 11.55°N, 76.57°E
Average Temperature 15°C to 35°C
Altitude Range 850 m to 1,250 m above sea level
Famous For Tigers, Elephants, Birds
Best Visit October – March

2. Kodaikanal – The Princess of Hill Stations


Surrounded by misty forests and waterfalls, Kodaikanal is famous for its peaceful Kodai Lake and breathtaking Coaker’s Walk views.


Kodaikanal

Quick Facts
Location Dindigul District, Tamil Nadu, India
Established 1845 (as a hill station by British missionaries)
Coordinates 10.23°N, 77.48°E
Nickname The Princess of Hill Stations
Famous For Lakes, Hills, Waterfalls
Average Temperature 8°C to 20°C
Elevation 2,133 m

4. Gulf of Mannar Marine National Park


A group of 21 coral islands between India and Sri Lanka, this park is rich in marine biodiversity with corals, seagrass, and mangroves.


Gulf of Mannar

Quick Facts
Location Ramanathapuram and Tuticorin Districts, Tamil Nadu, India
Established 1986
Total Area 560 sq. km (Marine National Park), part of 10,500 sq. km Biosphere Reserve
Coordinates 8.78°N, 79.13°E
Average Temperature 25°C to 35°C
Islands 21 Coral Islands
Marine Life Corals, Dolphins, Sea Cows

5. Vedanthangal Bird Sanctuary


One of India’s oldest bird sanctuaries, home to pelicans, egrets, and herons that migrate every winter.


Vedanthangal Bird Sanctuary

Quick Facts
Location Chengalpattu District, Tamil Nadu, India
Established 1936 (one of the oldest bird sanctuaries in India)
Coordinates 12.51°N, 79.86°E
Average Temperature 20°C to 35°C
Area ~30 hectares
Famous Species Pelicans, Egrets, Herons
Migratory Season November to March

6. Kalakkad–Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve


Tamil Nadu’s largest protected area, part of the Western Ghats, rich with tigers, elephants, and lush tropical forests.


Kalakkad–Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve

Quick Facts
Location Tirunelveli and Kanyakumari Districts, Tamil Nadu, India
Area 895 sq. km
Established 1988 (as a Tiger Reserve under Project Tiger)
Coordinates 8.53°N, 77.35°E
Average Temperature 20°C to 35°C
Climate Tropical to subtropical; varies with altitude
Annual Rainfall 1,500–2,500 mm (highest in Tamil Nadu)
