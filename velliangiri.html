Velliangiri Hills

The Sacred Himalayas of the South

🌄 Overview

Velliangiri Hills, often called the “Kailash of the South,” are located in Coimbatore district of Tamil Nadu, India. They are part of the Western Ghats and lie at the westernmost end of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve. The hills are spiritually significant, attracting pilgrims, and ecologically rich, serving as a habitat for diverse flora and fauna. The highest peak rises to about 1,520 meters above sea level.

📍 Location

  • State: Tamil Nadu
  • District: Coimbatore
  • Nearest Towns: Coimbatore (~40 km), Pollachi (~25 km)
  • Coordinates: ~10.7°N, 77°E

🌿 Biodiversity

The Velliangiri Hills are part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve and are home to tropical evergreen forests, shola forests, and grasslands.

Flora:

  • Tropical evergreen forests
  • Shola forests in higher altitudes
  • Medicinal plants used in Ayurveda
  • Endemic species like Strobilanthes kunthiana (Neelakurinji)

Fauna:

  • Mammals: Elephants, Nilgiri Langurs, Tigers (rare), Leopards
  • Birds: Malabar Grey Hornbill, Black Eagle, Crested Serpent Eagle
  • Reptiles & Amphibians: Various endemic snakes and frogs

🦁 Wildlife Highlights

  • Known for its rich elephant population and sightings of leopards
  • Sacred groves and undisturbed forests maintain ecological balance
  • Rare and endemic species make it a research hotspot

🌦️ Climate

  • Tropical climate with heavy rainfall during the southwest and northeast monsoons
  • Temperature: 20°C–30°C in lowlands, cooler at higher elevations
  • Best time to visit: October to March

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Air: Nearest airport – Coimbatore International Airport (~40 km)
  • By Train: Coimbatore Junction (~40 km)
  • By Road: Accessible from Coimbatore or Pollachi; trekking paths lead to peaks

🌿 Tourism & Activities

  • Pilgrimage trekking to Velliangiri Shiva Temple (especially during full moon festivals)
  • Nature trekking, wildlife photography, and birdwatching
  • Meditation and spiritual retreats in forested areas

💡 Conservation Importance

  • Part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve
  • Supports endemic species and sacred groves
  • Helps preserve watersheds feeding rivers like Bhavani and Amaravathi

📸 Fun Facts

  • Called “Kailash of the South” due to spiritual significance
  • Neelakurinji flowers bloom once every 12 years on the hills
  • Dense forests act as a carbon sink and biodiversity hotspot
