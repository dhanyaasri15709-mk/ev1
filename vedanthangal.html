Where Nature and Wings Unite in Harmony

Vedanthangal Bird Sanctuary is one of the oldest bird sanctuaries in India, located in the Kanchipuram district of Tamil Nadu, approximately 75 km from Chennai. Known for its rich avian diversity, the sanctuary is a haven for migratory and resident birds alike. üê¶ Birds & Wildlife

Vedanthangal is home to over 40,000 birds, including more than 200 species. Migratory birds arrive here mainly between November and March, attracted by the shallow waters and trees that provide safe nesting grounds.

  • Herons and Egrets (Grey Heron, Pond Heron, Cattle Egret)
  • Pelicans
  • Darters
  • Storks (Painted Stork, Openbill Stork)
  • Kingfishers
  • Cormorants
  • Moorhens and Waterfowls

üå≥ Ecological Importance

  • The wetland ecosystem here is crucial for the survival of migratory birds.
  • Provides breeding, feeding, and nesting habitats.
  • Acts as a natural water purification system.
  • Supports fish, amphibians, and other wetland organisms.
