Kalakkad–Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve

The Green Heart of Tamil Nadu’s Western Ghats

🌳 Overview

Kalakkad–Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve, commonly called KMTR, is a protected area in Tamil Nadu, India, located in the southern Western Ghats. Established in 1988, it is one of the largest tiger reserves in India and a biodiversity hotspot. The reserve spans across Tirunelveli and Kanyakumari districts, covering around 895 km².

📍 Location

State: Tamil Nadu
Districts: Tirunelveli & Kanyakumari
Nearest Towns: Kalakad (~10 km), Mundanthurai (~25 km), Tirunelveli (~55 km)
Coordinates: ~8.5°N, 77.3°E

🌿 Biodiversity

KMTR is part of the Agasthyamalai Biosphere Reserve, rich in endemic flora and fauna.

  • Flora: Tropical evergreen forests, semi-evergreen and deciduous forests, Shola-grassland mosaics in highlands, medicinal plants and endemic species of the Western Ghats
  • Fauna: Mammals - Bengal Tiger, Indian Elephant, Leopard, Nilgiri Tahr, Sloth Bear, Gaur; Birds - Malabar Grey Hornbill, Indian Peafowl, Crested Serpent Eagle; Reptiles & Amphibians - King Cobra, endemic frogs and snakes; Insects - Rich butterfly diversity

🦁 Wildlife Highlights

  • Tigers and elephants are the flagship species.
  • Nilgiri Tahr, an endangered mountain goat, inhabits the highlands.
  • Rare endemic amphibians and reptiles make it a research hotspot.

🌦️ Climate

Tropical climate with high rainfall due to southwest and northeast monsoons.
Temperature ranges: 20°C–35°C in lowlands, cooler in the highlands.
Best time to visit: November to May

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Air: Nearest airport – Tuticorin Airport (~80 km) or Trivandrum Airport (~100 km)
  • By Train: Nearest railway stations – Tirunelveli (~55 km) or Nagercoil (~90 km)
  • By Road: Well-connected from Tirunelveli, Nagercoil, and Kanyakumari

🌿 Tourism & Activities

  • Jungle safaris for tiger and elephant sightings
  • Bird watching (many endemic species)
  • Nature trekking and photography
  • Eco-tourism programs run by Tamil Nadu Forest Department

💡 Conservation Importance

  • Part of Project Tiger since 1988
  • Maintains wildlife corridors connecting Western Ghats to Kerala
  • Protects endangered species like Nilgiri Tahr, Bengal Tiger, and Asian Elephant

📸 Fun Facts

  • Home to rare medicinal plants studied for traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
  • Western Ghats in this region are UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
  • Forest cover is around 80%, making it one of the most biodiverse tiger reserves in India.
